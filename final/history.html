Brief History of Beaded Jewelry

Home > Fun Links > History

Ever wonder when the first beaded jewelry was created? Below is a brief look at the history of beaded jewelry.

In the Beginning...

According to the Antique Jewelry University timeline, beaded jewelry began circa 110,000 BC. Christie Romero and Barbara Talbott note, "Archaeological finds in Morocco tell us that shell beads were used as decorative objects; the oldest jewelry known to man. They may have served as amulets."

Shell and "bone beads" made from animal bones continued to be prominent over the next 100,000 year until around 3,600 BC, when colors became more common, with stones such as obsidian, lapis, and turquoise.

A few thousand years later precious and semi-precious stones were discovered and became more popular than beads in many cultures.


The bead, however, remains a popular aspect of Native American and tribal cultures. According to the American Museum of Natural History's Totems to Turquoise exhibit, "Long-standing cultural beliefs shape artists' choices of colors, designs and materials in both the Southwest and the Northwest Coast. Even the most modern creations are linked to deeply rooted ideas about the structure and meaning of the world."

Courtesy of Antique Jewelry University (AJU) and American Museum of Natural History (AMNH)


Learn More

The AMNH also oversees the Center for Bead Research, an authoritative site created by renowned bead expert Peter Francis, Jr. The site is part of the Division of Anthropology at the AMNH.

In addition to volumes of bead research, there are lighter articles and interactive items, like the Is It a Bead? quiz.
